Here is a list of classical music training scores for piano students, organized by grade from 1 to 10. This list includes representative pieces, their composers, and the series or publications where you can often find them. This should help you in your search for open-source scores.

### Understanding the Grading Systems

It is important to note that different examination boards, such as the Associated Board of the Royal Schools of Music (ABRSM) and the Royal Conservatory of Music (RCM), have their own grading systems. While there's no universal standard, the following lists provide a good representation of the progressive difficulty at each level. The "Masterwork Classics" series by Jane Magrath is another excellent, widely used graded repertoire series.

### Grade 1

At this level, pieces are simple, with basic rhythms and hand coordination.

**Representative Pieces:**

- **Anonymous:** A Toy (from _The Fitzwilliam Virginal Book_)
- **J.S. Bach:** Minuet in G Major (from _Anna Magdalena Bach's Notebook_)
- **Jeremiah Clarke:** King William's March
- **Stephen Duro:** Calypso Joe
- **Walter Carroll:** The Wood Fairies (from _Scenes at a Farm_)
- **Melodie by Schumann**
- **Jazzy Dragon by Barbara Snow**

**Recommended Publications:**

- ABRSM Piano Exam Pieces Grade 1
- Masterwork Classics, Level 1-2 (edited by Jane Magrath)

### Grade 2

Pieces at this level introduce more complex rhythms and coordination between the hands.

**Representative Pieces:**

- **Anton Diabelli:** Bagatelle
- **Alexander Reinagle:** Allegro in C Major, op. 1, no. 4
- **Dmitri Kabalevsky:** A Little Dance
- **Christopher Norton:** Inter-City Stomp
- **Joseph Haydn:** Rondino
- **Spindler:** Waltz in A Minor

**Recommended Publications:**

- ABRSM Piano Exam Pieces Grade 2
- Masterwork Classics, Level 1-2 (edited by Jane Magrath)

### Grade 3

This level introduces a broader key range and greater technical demands.

**Representative Pieces:**

- **Muzio Clementi:** Sonatina in C Major, Op. 36 No. 1 (1st Movement)
- **Robert Schumann:** The Wild Horseman (from _Album for the Young_, Op. 68, No. 8)
- **Béla Bartók:** Play (from _For Children_, Vol. 1)
- **L. Mozart:** Allegro in F
- **Tarantella Twist** (from Piano Grades Are Go!)

**Recommended Publications:**

- ABRSM Piano Exam Pieces Grade 3
- Masterwork Classics, Level 3 (edited by Jane Magrath)

### Grade 4

At this stage, there's an increase in complexity of scales, arpeggios, and musical expression.

**Representative Pieces:**

- **G.F. Handel:** Allegro in F, HWV 488
- **Valerie Capers:** Billie's Song

- **Pescetti:** Presto (3rd movt from Sonata No. 6 in C minor)
- **Farrenc:** Mouvement de valse (No. 15 from 25 études faciles, Op. 50)

**Recommended Publications:**

- ABRSM Piano Exam Pieces Grade 4
- Masterwork Classics, Level 4 (edited by Jane Magrath)

### Grade 5

This is an intermediate level with longer pieces that require a greater dynamic and technical range.

**Representative Pieces:**

- **J.S. Bach:** Invention No. 8 in F, BWV 779
- **Ludwig van Beethoven:** Bagatelle in G minor, Op. 119 No. 1
- **Robert Schumann:** Von fremden Ländern und Menschen (from _Kinderszenen_, Op. 15, No. 1)
- **Gillock:** Arabesque Sentimentale
- **Mompou:** La barca (from _Impresiones íntimas_)

**Recommended Publications:**

- ABRSM Piano Exam Pieces Grade 5
- Masterwork Classics, Level 5 (edited by Jane Magrath)

**Representative Pieces:**

### Grade 8

This is the highest of the standard graded exams, representing a pre-conservatoire level of proficiency.

**Representative Pieces:**

- **W.A. Mozart:** "Alla Turca", Third movement from Sonata KV 331

- **Franz Liszt:** Consolation in E, No. 2 from Six Consolations

**Recommended Publications:**

- Masterwork Classics, Level 8 (edited by Jane Magrath)

### Grade 9

Beyond Grade 8, the levels are often designated by diplomas or advanced certificates. The repertoire is more demanding, requiring mature musical understanding.

**Representative Pieces:**

- **J.S. Bach:** Invention in E Major, BWV 777
- **W.A. Mozart:** Sonata in C Major, K. 545 (First Movement)
- **Frédéric Chopin:** Mazurka in A Minor, Op. 67, No. 4

**Recommended Publications:**

- Conservatory Canada Grade 9 Repertoire

- Masterwork Classics, Level 9 (edited by Jane Magrath)

### Grade 10

This level represents early advanced to advanced repertoire, suitable for undergraduate music study.

**Representative Pieces:**

- **J.S. Bach:** Allemande and Courante from French Suite in G Major, BWV 816

- **Franz Joseph Haydn:** Sonata in E Minor, Hob. XVI:34
- **Heitor Villa-Lobos:** O Polichinelo

**Recommended Publications:**

- Royal Conservatory of Music (RCM) Grade 10 Repertoire
- Masterwork Classics, Level 10 (edited by Jane Magrath)

### Most Important Works for Piano Students

Here are some essential works that every serious piano student should aim to learn. The approximate grade levels are provided for reference.

- **J.S. Bach - The Well-Tempered Clavier:** (Grades 7-10 and beyond) A collection of preludes and fugues in all 24 major and minor keys. It is a cornerstone of the piano repertoire.
- **Ludwig van Beethoven - Piano Sonatas:** (Grades 6-10 and beyond) Especially the "Pathétique" (Op. 13), "Moonlight" (Op. 27, No. 2), and "Appassionata" (Op. 57).
- **Frédéric Chopin - Nocturnes, Waltzes, and Études:** (Grades 7-10 and beyond) These works are essential for developing a lyrical touch, rubato, and advanced technique.
- **Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart - Piano Sonatas:** (Grades 6-10) Particularly the Sonata in C Major, K. 545, which is a popular teaching piece.
- **Claude Debussy - Suite bergamasque:** (Grade 8 and beyond) This suite includes the famous "Clair de lune" and is a great introduction to impressionistic music.
- **Robert Schumann - Album for the Young, Op. 68 & Kinderszenen, Op. 15:** (Grades 3-7) These collections contain a wealth of character pieces that are excellent for developing musicality.
